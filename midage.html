MOVING ON

Seeing that his plea for him to continue his education fell on death ears; he told the relative he was living with then to allow him learn a trader to which to his surprise the request was granted. He was asked what trade he wishes to learn and he said carpentry. Enquiries were made as to where he can learn, a place was found and he was enrolled with one and his training resumed in earnest. He was steadfast in his training and excelled in the carpentry training so much that in no time, he became skilled in the trade and was already doing small jobs at home and in the community which was fetching him little stipends. Life seems so good but unknown to him, tragedy was about to rear its ugly head.

FATHER'S DEATH

He was at work one day when someone from home came and told his boss that Pa Ajani was needed at home urgently. Pa Bashir was curious as to what could have warranted them to come ask of him when he just left home not quite long ago. His then boss granted them the request for him to go home. He got home and met a mammoth crowd who were lamenting. He became apprehensive as to what could have happened but no one was willing to tell him a thing until he was summoned into a room and told that his father, Pa James Ogunshola was dead and that he (Pa Ajani) has to man up and take the mantle as the new head of the Ogunshola family as he was the first born son and he was to get married as soon as possible so as to continue the lineage
